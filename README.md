## Overview

This repository contains the materials associated with an independent research project that investigates cybersecurity awareness and online safety practices among high school and university students in Burkina Faso.

As internet access and smartphone usage continue to grow across the country, students are increasingly exposed to cyber threats such as phishing, malware, identity theft, online scams, weak password practices, and social engineering. Despite these risks, limited research has examined how well students understand cybersecurity and how they protect themselves online.

The objective of this study is to provide preliminary evidence that can support cybersecurity education initiatives and encourage safer digital practices among students.

---

## Research Objectives

The study aims to:

- assess students' cybersecurity awareness;
- evaluate their online safety practices;
- measure knowledge of common cyber threats;
- identify risky online behaviors;
- examine factors influencing cybersecurity awareness;
- provide practical recommendations for improving cybersecurity education in Burkina Faso.

---

## Research Questions

The study addresses the following questions:

1. What is the level of cybersecurity awareness among students?
2. How do students protect themselves while using the Internet?
3. Which cybersecurity threats are least understood?
4. What factors influence cybersecurity awareness?
5. How can cybersecurity education be strengthened?

- Quantitative descriptive study

Burkina Faso

### Sample Size

- 33 participants

The collected responses were analyzed using descriptive statistics, including:

- charts.

The questionnaire covered several cybersecurity domains, including:

---

## Key Findings

The study revealed several important observations.

- Most participants access the Internet several times a day.
- Smartphones are the primary device used to connect to the Internet.
- Most respondents have heard of cybersecurity.
- Knowledge of phishing and malware remains incomplete.
- Password reuse is still common.
- Website authenticity is not always verified before entering personal information.
- Many students continue to engage in risky online behaviors.
- Almost every participant expressed interest in learning more about cybersecurity.

These findings suggest that students recognize the importance of cybersecurity but still require practical education to develop safer online habits.

---

## Recommendations

Based on the findings, the study recommends:

- integrating cybersecurity education into school curricula;
- organizing cybersecurity awareness campaigns;
- promoting stronger password management;
- encouraging safer online behaviors;
- improving digital literacy programs;
- expanding cybersecurity training opportunities for students.
